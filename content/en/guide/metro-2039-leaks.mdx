## Technical Leap: Unreal Engine 5 and "Lumen" Lighting

One of the most exciting aspects of the **metro 2039 leaks** is the confirmation that the series has moved to Unreal Engine 5. For a franchise defined by its lighting and shadows, this move is transformative. 

Internal testing logs mention "Lumen" extensively. In one leaked test sequence, a single flickering candle in a rusted metro grate realistically illuminates the volumetric dust particles and the glistening, wet surfaces of the tunnel. This isn't just about graphics; it’s about maintaining the atmosphere that fans expect while achieving a level of fidelity that the old 4A engine simply couldn't support.

### New Gameplay Mechanics

The leak reveals several innovative systems that distinguish Metro 2039 from its predecessors:

1.  **Radio Echo:** This mechanic transforms the ruins of Moscow into a "psychic playground." It allows Artyom to tune into the lingering memories of the dead, uncovering hidden paths or lore fragments through audio distortions.
2.  **Ultrasonic Crafting:** Moving beyond simple battery management, players can now craft ultrasonic emitters. These tools are essential for warding off specific mutant types, such as spiders, without wasting precious ammunition.
3.  **Environmental Degradation:** The leak suggests that gas mask filters are no longer the only timed resource. Artyom’s suit and weapons now undergo visible and functional wear-and-tear based on the acid rain and radiation levels encountered.
