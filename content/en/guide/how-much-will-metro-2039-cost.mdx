## Weapon Customization and Realism

A major part of the *Metro 2039* experience is the "trash gun" system. Unlike *Fallout 4*, where homemade guns look like toys, *Metro 2039* guns have a heavy, industrial feel. The cost of the game reflects the high-fidelity assets used for these weapons. You will see wheels, gas tubes, and pneumatic pumps integrated into standard AK frames.

While some experts argue that a "basic AK would be more sensible," the developers have doubled down on the steampunk aesthetic to differentiate this entry from *Exodus*. This visual polish is one of the reasons why the development budget—and subsequently the retail price—has increased compared to previous entries in the series.

## FAQ

**Q: How much will metro 2039 cost on Steam vs. Console?**

A: The base price is expected to be **$69.99** across all platforms (Steam, PS5, and Xbox Series X/S) for the Standard Edition. However, Steam users may see more frequent sales or "Loyalty Discounts" if they own previous games like *Metro 2033* or *Metro Exodus*.

**Q: Is there a free upgrade for next-gen consoles?**

A: Since *Metro 2039* is being built specifically for current-gen hardware (PS5/Xbox Series X) and high-end PCs, there is no "cross-gen" upgrade path. The game will not be available on PS4 or Xbox One.

**Q: When is the Metro 2039 release date?**

A: The game is officially scheduled for a "Winter 2026" release. Pre-orders are expected to go live approximately three to four months before the launch date.

**Q: Will Metro 2039 have microtransactions?**

A: 4A Games has traditionally avoided microtransactions in the Metro series, focusing instead on large-scale story expansions included in a Season Pass or sold separately. Expect a "buy once, play forever" model for the base campaign.
