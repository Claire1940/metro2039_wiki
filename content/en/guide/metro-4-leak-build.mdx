## Protagonists and Narrative Shift

One of the most shocking revelations from the **metro 4 leak build** is the absence of Artyom as a playable character. Instead, the narrative was centered around Hunter, the Spartan Ranger whose disappearance sparked the events of the first game. The leaked dialogue and notes indicate that the game would have taken place between the years 2033 and 2034, aligning closely with the events of the second book in the series.

### Key Characters in the Scrapped Build

The leak highlights a return of fan-favorite characters, some of whom were thought to have smaller roles or different fates in the established game lore.

*   **Hunter:** The primary protagonist. The leak reveals a deeper backstory, including the existence of a daughter, which adds a layer of personal stakes to his survival in the wastes.
*   **Khan:** A major supporting character with extensive dialogue. His role seems to involve guiding Hunter through both the physical and spiritual dangers of the Metro.
*   **Bourbon:** Surprisingly, Bourbon appears with an "upgraded status." While details are vague, his presence suggests a more prominent role in the underground economy or faction politics.
*   **Artyom:** Present only as a "lore character." He reportedly has no direct dialogue in this build, serving as a living legend within the Metro rather than the player's avatar.

## Open World Mechanics and Exploration

The **metro 4 leak build** suggests that 4A Games was moving toward a "fully open world" structure. Unlike the "sandbox hubs" found in *Metro Exodus*, this scrapped version aimed to allow players to travel between various Metro stations and the surface at will.

### Exploration Features:
1.  **Station Interaction:** Players could enter inhabited stations, interact with occupants, and build or break relationships with different factions.
2.  **Unbound Exploration:** The leak shows screenshots of seamless transitions between the classic underground tunnels and the irradiated surface.
3.  **Dynamic Factions:** Familiar groups like the Red Line and the Fourth Reich (Nazis) appear as active forces that the player must navigate.

> **💡 Tip:** In the scrapped build, relationship building was a core mechanic. Actions taken in one station could potentially lock out quests or resources in another, emphasizing the political tension of the Metro.
